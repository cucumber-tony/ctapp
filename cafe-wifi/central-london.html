Central London Venues

Dean Street Townhouse, Soho

(26-29 Dean Street, London, W1D 3LL)

Brilliant Winter spot, the low ceilings and dark furnishing mean you can find a corner & be undisturbed for hours. In the heart of Soho, would make excellent business meeting location. Book a comfy chair & enjoy their cocktails. Upscale brunch & special offers for Soho house members.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 1/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

Blind Pig, Soho

(58-59 Poland Street, London W1F 7NS)

Quite pricey, Brilliant menu. The Mood suits afternoon to late evening meetings. The cocktails look amazing. Close to the station.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

TimberYard, Covent Garden

(7 Upper St Martin’s Lane, Seven Dials, WC2H 9DL)

This combines brunch with an atmosphere of creative people as a Hipster hangout. Try the chocolate & peanut butter brownies. The overall scenery is for casual/informal focus groups and personal lunches. It is health conscious and perfect for Coffee.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 5/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 5/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

The Club House, Mayfair

(11-14 Grafton Street, Mayfair, London W1S 4EW)

Sophisticated, quiet spot perfect for corporate meetings, access to meeting rooms. Professional setting that promotes networking, overall Member’s only. Upscale area & furnishing, perfect for celebrations that require access to Internet.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 5/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 5/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

Riding House Café, Hoxton

(43-51 Great Titchfield St, London W1W 7PQ)

Just at the edge of East London, it’s a secret escape from the hustle & bustle of Oxford Street. The natural light makes it a perfect setting for long days typing & staring out into London streets. Brilliant atmosphere to bring clients, an appetizing brunch menu as well as Tea & cakes to choose from. Includes a private meeting room, if you need to seek more silence in groups.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 5/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

Raw Press

(32 Dover Street, London W1S 4NE)

If you love fresh juices & healthy filling salads this is the place. This quirky setting is perfect for health conscious clients or someone looking for organic food at a reasonable price. Remember to enter through the Wolf & Badger store to get to the cafe.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 4/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

Welcome Collection

(183 Euston Rd, London)

This is combined with the Welcome Collection museum, very bright & spacious. The atmosphere is many different people having meetings, be wary of the times you choose, as it tends to get busy despite its huge size. The menu is reasonably priced and is a combination of Salads, Cakes & Pastries.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 2/5

Flat Planet

(39 Great Marlborough St, W1F 7JG)

Organic menu, notorious for their amazing pizza. Perfect spot for finding solace with headphones, 2 floors and their menu is simple but brilliant. Reasonably priced with a wide range of gluten free options, and a very popular home-made lemonade.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 2/5

Department of Coffee and Social Affairs

(14-16 Leather Lane, EC1N 7SU)

This is a small but cozy, various cakes and amazing coffee. Committed to non-profit organizations, can get busy and is best for work lunches of no more than 2-3. Lack of charging points mean it’s ideal for swift meetings.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 1/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5
