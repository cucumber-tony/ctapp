East London Venues

Forge & Co, Shoreditch

(154-158 Shoreditch High St, E1 6HU)

Defines itself ‘unique social workspace’ this is a renovated old building. So it’s modern with a classic mystique. There are 5 floors, including an art gallery visually brilliant for creative meeting and potential clients. They also have fresh healthy juices as well as coffee.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 4/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

Love shake, Dalston

(5 Kingsland Road E2 8 AA)

Better for milkshakes than coffee, great reasonably priced menu. Best to go early and enjoy the lighting, can become quite dark & loud. Ideal for short stay, Try the Peanut butter & Oreo milkshake. The whole setting is close to the station, and it perfect for freelancers.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 1/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 1/5

Ace Hotel, Shoreditch

(100 Shoreditch High Street, London, E1 6JQ)

Visually combines business with cool, a various range of healthy juices to keep you feeling refreshed. Hoi Polloi has power sockets at every table, so ideal for focus groups and meetings. Perfect for integrating food and work.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 5/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

The Laundry, London fields

(2-18 Warburton Road, London, E8 3FN)

Interestingly owned by the guitarist of Radiohead. Ideal for combining drinks with work, or taking a potential client. The spacious décor makes it naturally light and perfect for those who enjoy listening to music as they work. The setting is brilliant for networking and it close to the station.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 5/5
Power Points: ?/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Shoreditch house, Shoreditch

(Ebor Street, Shoreditch, London, E1 6AW)

Ultra-exclusive and perfect for networking. This is ideal for freelancers who want the opportunity to work along elite London. There is a monthly membership and an upscale menu. It’s known as a celebrity hotspot.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 5/5
Power Points: 3/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

Look Mum No Hands, Old street

(49 Old Street, London, EC1V 9HX)

This one is great if you’re an avid cyclist, defines itself as a ‘café/workshop’ so it’s easy and comfortable to spend hours on end there. There is a eco-hipster persona to the establishment, and a range of places to plug yourself in.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 5/5
Power Points: 3/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

Hoxton Hotel, Old street

(81 Great Eastern St, London EC2A 3HU)

This is less busy during weekdays, and a spacious and cozy environment. The Menu is a tad expensive considering it’s simplicity, but the atmosphere is great and its perfect for working in all day, then drinking in the evening. If you want to be left alone submerge into your headphones without distraction it’s perfect.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: ?/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

Google Campus, Old Street

(4-5 Bonhill Street, London EC2A 4BX)

This is ideal for group innovation; you can bring people and work in groups. This means like-minded people getting work done surround you. The feeling of being inside busy but focused. It’s perfect for being alone or focus groups.

WiFi: 3/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 5/5
Coffee/Menu: 2/5

The Book Club, Shoreditch

(100-106 Leonard St, London EC2A 4RH)

This is perfect for early morning meetings; they have Life drawing classes and Informative talks that take place so check out what they have planned. It’s open, bright and during weekdays doesn’t get overbearingly busy.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 5/5
Coffee/Menu: 2/5

Foxcroft & Ginger

(68-89 Mile End Rd, Whitechapel, Greater London, E1 4TT)

This has a trendy setting, with an industrial feel to it. The staffs are friendly and encourage people to do work inside. The coffee is great and the Breakfast menu is popular. This is ideal students or workers, there is a D-I-Y element to the décor that make you feel informal and comfortable.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5
