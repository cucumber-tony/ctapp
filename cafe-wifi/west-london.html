West London Venues

The Electric, Notting Hill

(191 Portobello Road, London, W11 2ED)

This Italian-American spot has a great setting, close to the station and its high booths and diner seats means you can have formal meeting to all-day sit ins. The pristine décor means it's ideal for bringing and meeting people, the prices are steep but you can get many reasonable sides & drinks.

WiFi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 4/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

The Imperial 577, Fulham

(191 Portobello Rd, Notting Hill, London W11 2ED)

BThis is a classic setting that has been renovated to suit the modern aesthetic high St London offers. The pricing is pretty steep but the drinks & small bites are reasonable. This is good for a few hours work, if you want calm and natural light it’s perfect. It’s mainly suitors having Lunch or Dinner, but it’s lovely for a spring afternoon.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: ?/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Beaufort House, Chelsea

(354 Kings road, Chelsea SW3 5UZ)

This has meeting rooms and a Brunch menu that is popular, upscale and perfect to bring someone for a meeting. The mood is more diners than people on Laptops. It’s ideal for groups, meetings and focus groups you can book meeting rooms as well as it being child friendly.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: ?/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Paradise By Way of Kensal Green

(19 Kilburn Ln, London W10 4AE)

This is an upscale gastro-diner that prides itself on British cuisine. The pricing is pretty mid-range, and it’s most notorious meal is a favorable Sunday lunch. It’s perfect to escape the busy streets of London; it has outdoor seating that has a lovely setting where you could sit all day.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: ?/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Joe And The Juice

(65-67 Broadwick Street, W1F 9QY)

Situated in the heart of Central, this is perfect to avoiding the crowds and seeking some solace. They have a different branch with cozy sofas. It’s small so can get busy quickly but it amazing in the Coffee department and for small lunches.

WiFi: 5/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 3/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Minkies Deli

(Chamberlayne Rd, NW10 5RQ)

This Deli has a range of options for food & drink including a make-your-own Breakfast. The setting is light and spacious with a combination of outdoor and indoor seating. This is ideal for solitude work to be done somewhere you can admire the street from inside.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

The Tea & Coffee Plant

(180 Portobello Rd, W11 2EB)

This small, yet cozy Café is perfect if you like your coffee. Their Colombian fair trade coffee is popular. Their menu ranges from organic, to gourmet and even herbal teas. It’s Ideal for a few hours, but not somewhere to bring clients or large groups of people. They serve toasted sandwiches and an array of brilliant snacks.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 1/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

The Troubadour

(263-265 Old Brompton Rd, SW 9JA)

This is a historical place that has its trendy roots in the 1950’s Bohemian era, Bob Dylan’s played his first London gig here. You can hire out private rooms, and its Menu is reasonably priced. This is ideal for work meeting that can spill over into drinks, there is regularly live music so it’s best to go in the afternoon/morning if your looking for calm, quiet surroundings.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

The Westbourne

(101 Westbourne Park Villas, W2 5ED)

This is perfect for late after-noon/evening meetings, its down to earth persona in the midst of Notting Hill means it has a reasonably priced menu, it can get busy on weekends. The setting is perfect to hold group meetings, and the staffs are welcoming. The drinks range from Belgian beers to coffee, the menu changes daily and is made from fresh food from independent suppliers.

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Camden Arts Centre Café

(Arkwright Rd, NW3 6DG)

Time out describes this venue as ‘best coffee in West London’ so it’s perfect for coffee that will keep you energized throughout the day. It’s on the edge of Camden and you can work outside alongside artwork. There is also a gallery and their Pastries are popular!

WiFi: 2/5
Service: 4/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 3/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5
